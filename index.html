माया The Divine Illusion

Understand that the material world is a temporary illusion (Maya) that masks the eternal soul.

LESSON 1
The Foundation

The Upside-Down Tree

अश्वत्थं — Ashvattha

In the Bhagavad Gita (Chapter 15), Krishna describes the material world as an inverted banyan tree — the Ashvattha. Its roots grow upward into the Divine, while its branches extend downward into the material world.

🌳

Roots Above

The roots represent Brahman — the ultimate reality and source of all creation.

🍃

Branches Below

The branches are our senses, desires, and attachments that pull us toward worldly existence.

👁️

The Leaves

The leaves are the Vedic hymns — knowledge that sustains the tree of existence.

The Law of Action

The Wheel of Karma

कर्म — Action & Reaction

Karma literally means action. It is the universal principle of cause and effect. Every action, word, and thought creates an energetic imprint that returns to us.

🌱

As You Sow

Your present reality is the harvest of your past actions. You are the architect of your destiny.

🔄

The Endless Cycle

Unresolved karma binds the soul to Samsara — the continuous cycle of birth, death, and rebirth.

The Vault of Actions

Three Kinds of Karma

संचित • प्रारब्ध • आगामी

Karma is not just happening now; it's a vast repository spanning timelines. Tap the vessels to see how they work.

SanchitaThe Vast Reservoir

The accumulated sum of all past actions from countless lifetimes. It is the vast warehouse of pending karma, most of which is dormant.

PrarabdhaBearing Fruit Now

The portion of Sanchita karma chosen for this specific lifetime. It dictates your body, family, and inevitable life situations. You cannot change it, only how you react to it.

AgamiFuture Creations

The new karma you are generating right now through your current choices. This is where your free will exists.
